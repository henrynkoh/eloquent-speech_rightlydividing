## Day 1: Complete Walkthrough

### Step-by-Step: Your First Lesson

**Time Estimate**: 30-45 minutes total

#### Part 1: Reading the Lesson (15-20 minutes)

**Step 1: Read the Overview** (2 minutes)
- Scroll to the "Overview" section
- Read the full paragraph slowly
- Ask yourself: "What will I learn today?"
- Take one note: Write down the main goal

**Step 2: Study the Key Points** (5 minutes)
- Go through each bullet point
- Pause after each one
- Ask: "How does this apply to me?"
- Highlight or note the points that resonate most

**Key Points to Focus On**:
1. Eliminating filler words
2. Mastering posture
3. Eye contact techniques
4. Powerful openings
5. Speaking from conviction

**Step 3: Read the Biblical Foundation** (3 minutes)
- Read the verse out loud (this helps memory)
- Read the explanation
- Consider: "How does this change my perspective?"
- If the verse is new, look it up in context

**Step 4: Study Rightly Dividing** (5 minutes)
- This is the deep learning section
- Read slowly - don't rush
- The concept: Law vs. Grace in Communication
- Understand: Law seeks approval, Grace speaks from approval
- This is foundational - take your time

**Step 5: Review the Practice Exercise** (2 minutes)
- Read the exercise title
- Skim the instructions (you'll do them next)
- Note what you'll need (recording device, etc.)

#### Part 2: Practice Exercise (10-15 minutes)

**Exercise 1: Record Your 1-Minute Vision**

**Setup (2 minutes)**:
1. Get your phone or camera
2. Set it up on a stable surface (or use a tripod)
3. Stand in front of it (don't sit - posture matters)
4. Ensure good lighting (face the light source)
5. Set a timer for exactly 60 seconds

**Preparation (2 minutes)**:
1. Think about your startup vision
2. Write down 3-4 key points:
   - What problem are you solving?
   - Who are you helping?
   - What makes you different?
   - What's the vision for the future?

**First Recording (2 minutes)**:
1. Start the timer
2. Start recording
3. Deliver your vision in exactly 60 seconds
4. Stop recording when timer goes off
5. Don't worry about perfection - this is baseline

**Review (3 minutes)**:
1. Watch your recording (yes, it's uncomfortable - do it anyway)
2. Count filler words: Write down every "um," "uh," "like"
3. Check posture: Are you standing tall? Slouching?
4. Check eye contact: Are you looking at camera? Away?
5. Check voice: Clear? Confident? Or permission-seeking?

**Second Recording (3 minutes)**:
1. Based on your review, make improvements
2. Eliminate the filler words you identified
3. Fix your posture
4. Maintain eye contact with camera
5. Speak with conviction, not permission-seeking
6. Record again
7. Compare to first recording - you should see improvement

**Exercise 2: Refine Your 30-Second Intro**

**Setup (1 minute)**:
- Same setup as Exercise 1
- Timer set for 30 seconds

**Write It First (2 minutes)**:
- Craft your intro on paper or notes app
- Structure: Hook (5 sec) + Value (15 sec) + Differentiator (10 sec)
- Example structure:
  - Hook: "I help [target audience] [achieve outcome]"
  - Value: "We do this by [method] which results in [benefit]"
  - Differentiator: "What makes us unique is [unique value]"

**Practice Out Loud (2 minutes)**:
- Say it 3 times before recording
- Time yourself each time
- Cut unnecessary words
- Strengthen weak phrases

**Record and Refine (3 minutes)**:
1. Record your first attempt
2. Watch it back
3. Note what to improve
4. Record again
5. Keep refining until it's natural and confident

**Success Checklist**:
- ✅ No filler words
- ✅ Strong, confident posture
- ✅ Steady eye contact
- ✅ Clear, projected voice
- ✅ Speaking with conviction
- ✅ Exactly 30 seconds (or close)

#### Part 3: Reflection and Notes (5 minutes)

**Journal These Questions**:
1. What was my biggest insight from today's lesson?
2. What filler words do I use most? (List them)
3. How does speaking from grace (already approved) vs. law (seeking approval) change my approach?
4. What will I practice tomorrow?
5. What questions do I still have?

**Action Items for Tomorrow**:
- [ ] Practice eliminating [your most common filler word]
- [ ] Use my refined intro in a real conversation
- [ ] Remind myself: "I'm already approved, I speak from that place"

## Day 2: Complete Walkthrough

### Step-by-Step: Boundaries, Feedback & Conflict

**Time Estimate**: 30-45 minutes total

#### Part 1: Reading the Lesson (15-20 minutes)

**Step 1: Read Overview** (2 minutes)
- Focus on: Why boundaries matter for founders
- Note: How this builds on Day 1's confidence

**Step 2: Study Key Points** (5 minutes)
- Pay special attention to:
  - How to say "no" with clarity and kindness
  - The feedback framework: Observation + Impact + Request
  - De-escalation techniques

**Step 3: Biblical Foundation** (3 minutes)
- Colossians 4:6: "Let your speech be alway with grace, seasoned with salt"
- Understand: Grace doesn't mean weakness - it means truth with love

**Step 4: Rightly Dividing** (5 minutes)
- Concept: Spirit-Led vs. Law-Based Responses
- Key insight: Law responds with rules, Spirit responds with wisdom
- Application: Let the Spirit guide when to speak and when to be silent

**Step 5: Review Practice Exercises** (2 minutes)
- You'll role-play rejecting bad terms
- You'll practice delivering tough feedback
- Prepare mentally for these exercises

#### Part 2: Practice Exercises (10-15 minutes)

**Exercise 1: Role-Play Rejecting Bad Terms**

**Setup (2 minutes)**:
1. Find a practice partner (co-founder, friend, or use mirror)
2. Set scenario: Investor offers terms that don't align
3. Set timer for 5 minutes
4. Decide who goes first

**Role-Play (5 minutes)**:
1. Partner plays investor: Makes offer with pressure tactics
   - "This is our best offer"
   - "We need an answer today"
   - "Other investors are interested"
2. You practice saying no:
   - Use phrases from the lesson
   - Stay calm and confident
   - Be clear but kind
3. Switch roles and repeat

**Debrief (2 minutes)**:
- What worked?
- What felt awkward?
- What would you do differently?

**Exercise 2: Deliver Tough Feedback**

**Setup (2 minutes)**:
1. Write a scenario: Team member underperforming
2. Use specific example (not vague)
3. Focus on behavior, not character

**Practice the Framework (3 minutes)**:
1. **Observation**: "I noticed that [specific behavior]..."
   - Example: "I noticed you've arrived 15-20 minutes late to our last three team meetings."
2. **Impact**: "This affects [specific outcome]..."
   - Example: "This means we're starting late and losing productive time."
3. **Request**: "I'd like to see [specific change]..."
   - Example: "I'd like to see you arrive on time going forward. Is there something preventing you from being on time that we can address?"

**Record Yourself (3 minutes)**:
1. Record yourself delivering the feedback
2. Watch it back
3. Check: Is it clear? Kind? Actionable?
4. Refine and record again

#### Part 3: Reflection (5 minutes)

**Journal**:
1. What's hardest for me: Saying no or giving feedback?
2. How does "grace seasoned with salt" apply to my communication?
3. When do I need to let the Spirit guide my response?
4. What boundary do I need to set this week?

## Practice Exercise Tutorials

### How to Record Yourself Effectively

**Equipment Setup**:
1. Use your phone (most convenient)
2. Set up on stable surface or tripod
3. Position at eye level (not looking down)
4. Ensure good lighting (face the light)
5. Check audio (speak at normal volume, test first)

**Recording Tips**:
- Record in landscape (wider view)
- Stand 3-4 feet from camera
- Look at camera lens, not screen
- Speak naturally, don't perform
- Record multiple takes (first is rarely best)

**Review Process**:
1. Watch once without judgment (just observe)
2. Watch again with checklist (filler words, posture, etc.)
3. Note 3 things to improve
4. Record again with improvements
5. Compare before/after

### How to Practice with a Partner

**Finding a Partner**:
- Co-founder (best option - they benefit too)
- Team member
- Friend or family member
- Online practice group
- Mirror (if no partner available)

**Setting Up Practice Sessions**:
1. Schedule regular times
2. Set clear expectations
3. Agree on feedback style
4. Create safe space (no judgment)
5. Take turns practicing

**Giving Feedback**:
- Be specific ("You used 'um' 5 times" not "You seemed nervous")
- Be constructive ("Try pausing here" not "That was bad")
- Be encouraging ("Great improvement on posture" not just criticism)
- Focus on behavior, not character

**Receiving Feedback**:
- Listen fully (don't defend)
- Ask clarifying questions
- Take notes
- Thank them
- Apply the feedback

## Recording and Review Tutorials

### Setting Up Your Recording Space

**Location**:
- Quiet room (minimize background noise)
- Good lighting (natural light best, face the window)
- Neutral background (not distracting)
- Enough space to stand and move

**Camera Position**:
- Eye level (not looking up or down)
- 3-4 feet away (full upper body visible)
- Centered (you in middle of frame)
- Stable (tripod or stable surface)

**Audio Setup**:
- Quiet environment
- Speak at normal volume
- Test audio before recording
- Consider external mic if available

### Reviewing Your Recordings

**First Watch: Overall Impression**:
- Don't take notes yet
- Just watch and feel
- What's your gut reaction?
- What stands out (good or bad)?

**Second Watch: Technical Analysis**:
- Filler words: Count them, list them
- Posture: Slouching? Confident?
- Eye contact: Steady? Looking away?
- Voice: Clear? Confident? Too quiet?
- Pace: Too fast? Too slow? Just right?

**Third Watch: Content Analysis**:
- Message: Clear? Confusing?
- Structure: Logical flow?
- Engagement: Would you listen to this?
- Authenticity: Real or performing?

**Creating Improvement Plan**:
1. List top 3 issues
2. Prioritize (what's easiest to fix first?)
3. Set specific goals ("Eliminate 'um' from next recording")
4. Practice one thing at a time
5. Record again and compare

### Comparing Recordings Over Time

**Day 1 vs. Day 5 Comparison**:
1. Watch Day 1 recording first
2. Note baseline: What were you like?
3. Watch Day 5 recording
4. List all improvements (be specific)
5. Celebrate progress!

**Monthly Comparisons**:
- Record yourself monthly
- Compare to previous month
- Track improvement over time
- Adjust practice based on progress

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "I Don't Have Time"

**Solution**: Start with 15 minutes
- Read lesson: 10 minutes
- Quick practice: 5 minutes
- Better than skipping entirely
- Build habit first, then expand time

### "Recording Makes Me Nervous"

**Solution**: Start private
- Don't share recordings initially
- Remember: Everyone looks awkward at first
- Focus on improvement, not perfection
- It gets easier with practice

### "I Don't Understand the Biblical Concepts"

**Solution**: Focus on application
- Don't get stuck on theology
- Focus on: "How does this help me communicate?"
- Main point: Speak from grace (approved), not law (seeking approval)
- Ask questions if needed

### "Exercises Don't Apply to Me"

**Solution**: Adapt them
- Modify for your situation
- Use the principles, change the scenario
- Create your own exercises using the frameworks
- Focus on skills, not specific situations

### "I'm Not Seeing Improvement"

**Solution**: Compare recordings
- Watch Day 1 vs. current
- You'll see progress (even if small)
- Get feedback from others
- Be patient - real change takes time
